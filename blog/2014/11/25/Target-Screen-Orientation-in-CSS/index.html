Target Screen Orientation in CSS

I learned something new about CSS recently. It’s possible to target an @media query to the screen’s orientation:

Although this example is trivial (and super annoying), the idea was useful in my team’s capstone project. We’re making a web app to measure and track the rehabilitation progress of people with limited range of motion. In the least, we hope saves patients a visit to the doctor. The app records range of motion while a phone is strapped to their wrist or ankle. Problem: the strap fits over the middle of the screen, obstructing important indicators during the motion test. Cue the orientation targeting. A special .only-in-landscape class forces Bootstrap columns to full-width. Now, the instructions and progress bar stack vertically in portrait, but horizontally in landscape.
