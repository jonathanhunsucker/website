Visual understanding of the Pythangorean theorem

On May 12, 2019 by Jonathan Hunsucker

The Pythagorean theorem can be derived visually, from the following diagram, constructed by drawing:

  1. A right triangle with bases a and b, forming hypotenuse c
  2. Three squares: one from each of the triangle’s sides
  3. One last square, with side length a+b, surrounding the c-length square

With this in place, we can see that the area of the last square is (a+b)^2, which consists of the c-length square, and four right triangles with sides a and b. Altogether:

(a+b)^2 = 4(1/2*ab) + c^2
a^2 + 2ab + b^2 = 2ab + c^2
a^2 + b^2 = c^2
